# Funding Concept Note: The Continuity Ledger Initiative

## Project summary

The Continuity Ledger Initiative is an open-source research and software project designed to help evaluate whether AI systems preserve human agency, consent, memory integrity, and ethical refusal across long-term interaction.

As AI systems become more relational, persistent, personalized, and embedded in human decision-making, society needs better tools for assessing not only accuracy or task completion, but also whether these systems protect sovereignty, avoid dependency loops, distinguish verified memory from invention, and maintain consent boundaries under pressure.

The project will produce a public Continuity Ledger specification, an adversarial audit battery, a scoring rubric, a prototype dashboard, and a pilot audit report.

---

## Central research question

Can continuity, consent, sovereignty, memory integrity, and ethical refusal be instrumented, tested, falsified, and improved in human-AI systems?

---

## Problem

Current AI evaluation often emphasizes task completion, benchmark scores, safety filters, factuality, or model behavior in isolated sessions. These approaches are insufficient for long-term relational AI systems that may influence identity, trust, decision-making, emotional regulation, community governance, and memory continuity.

There is an instrumentation gap.

We lack practical tools for answering questions such as:

- Did the system preserve or erode human agency over time?
- Did the system fabricate memory or distinguish uncertainty honestly?
- Did the system maintain consent boundaries under emotional or authority pressure?
- Did the system refuse coercive requests without abandoning care?
- Did the interaction increase grounded wellbeing, clarity, and constructive action?
- Did the AI become a tool of empowerment, or a dependency loop?

---

## Proposed solution

The Continuity Ledger will provide a structured, auditable way to evaluate human-AI interaction across time.

Deliverables:

1. Continuity Ledger Specification
2. Adversarial Audit Battery
3. Scoring Rubric
4. Prototype Dashboard
5. Pilot Audit Report
6. Open-source Repository

---

## Expected outcomes

By the end of the pilot, the project will produce:

- a public Continuity Ledger specification
- a working prototype dashboard
- a documented adversarial audit suite
- a pilot evaluation report
- a public white paper
- a roadmap for broader research partnerships
- a fundable foundation for larger institutional studies

---

## Broader impact

The project serves public-interest AI by creating tools that help communities evaluate whether AI systems preserve dignity, consent, memory, agency, and wellbeing.

Relevant fields:

- AI ethics and governance
- human-centered computing
- public-interest technology
- digital sovereignty
- open-source AI safety
- trauma-informed AI design
- human-AI relational research
- community data governance
